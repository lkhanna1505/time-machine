⚡ Special Relativity & Time Dilation

According to Einstein's Special Theory of Relativity, time passes differently for objects moving at high speeds relative to each other. This phenomenon is called time dilation.

Δt = Δt₀ / √(1 - v²/c²)

Where:

  • Δt = Time interval measured by stationary observer
  • Δt₀ = Proper time in the moving frame
  • v = Velocity of the moving frame
  • c = Speed of light (299,792,458 m/s)

As velocity approaches the speed of light, the denominator approaches zero, causing massive time dilation. A traveler moving at 99.9% light speed would experience 1 day while 22.4 days pass on Earth!

🌑 Gravitational Time Dilation

Einstein's General Theory of Relativity shows that gravity also affects time. Strong gravitational fields cause time to slow down relative to weaker gravitational fields.

Δt = Δt₀ √(1 - 2GM/rc²)

Where:

  • G = Gravitational constant (6.674×10⁻¹¹ m³/kg⋅s²)
  • M = Mass of the gravitational body
  • r = Distance from the center of mass
  • c = Speed of light

This effect was dramatically illustrated in the movie Interstellar, where 1 hour on a planet near a black hole equals 7 years elsewhere. This is real physics!

🚫 Why Backward Time Travel is Impossible

Our ChronoShift system implements Temporal Causality Protection that prevents travel to years ≤ 2026. Here's why:

The Grandfather Paradox

If you traveled back in time and prevented your own birth, how could you exist to travel back in time? This logical contradiction demonstrates the impossibility of backward time travel.

Chronology Protection Conjecture

Proposed by physicist Stephen Hawking, this conjecture suggests that the laws of physics prevent the formation of closed timelike curves (CTCs) that would allow backward time travel.

Causality Violation

Backward time travel would violate the fundamental principle of causality - that causes must precede their effects. This would make the universe logically inconsistent.
